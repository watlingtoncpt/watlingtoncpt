Who we are

We are Watlington Community Property Limited, a Community Benefit Society in the process of registration with the Financial Conduct Authority. Our purpose is to acquire and manage commercial property in Watlington for the long-term benefit of the local community.

We are the data controller for all personal data you provide to us. This means we are responsible for deciding how and why your data is used, and for keeping it safe.

What data we collect and why

We only collect data that is necessary for the purpose for which you provide it. The table below explains what we collect, why, and on what legal basis.

Why you're sharing data with usData we collectLegal basis
Registering your interest in the project via our websiteName, email addressYour consent — you can withdraw this at any time

How we use your data

We use your data only for the purpose for which you provided it. We will never sell your data or share it with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

If you have registered an interest or signed up for updates, we will use your name and email address to send you information about WCP's progress, the share offer, and community events in Watlington. You can unsubscribe at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link in any email we send, or by emailing us at secretary@watlingtoncpt.org.uk.

Who we share your data with

We use the following services to help us communicate and manage information. These services process your data on our behalf and are required to keep it secure and use it only for the purposes we specify.

ServiceWhat it's used for
Mailchimp (Intuit Inc.)Sending newsletters and updates to supporters who have signed up
Google Forms / Google WorkspaceCollecting expressions of interest for Management Committee positions; our email service
Crowdfunder Community Shares (Crowdfunder Ltd)Processing share offer applications and payments from investors

Both services operate data processing agreements compliant with UK GDPR. Where data is transferred outside the UK, appropriate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses) are in place.

How long we keep your data

  • Newsletter subscribers and registered supporters: until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent, or after three years of inactivity — whichever is sooner.
  • Management Committee applicants: until the recruitment process is complete, then deleted unless you become a committee member.
  • Members: for the duration of your membership and for six years afterwards, as required by our Rules and financial record-keeping obligations.
  • General correspondence: for three years from the date of the correspondence.

Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Ask us to correct any inaccurate data
  • Ask us to delete your data in certain circumstances
  • Withdraw consent at any time where we rely on consent to process your data
  • Object to processing based on our legitimate interests
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at secretary@watlingtoncpt.org.uk. We will respond within one calendar month.

Cookies

Our website may use cookies to improve your experience. We will update this notice with full cookie information once our website is fully operational. We will always ask for your consent before setting any non-essential cookies.

How to complain

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal data, please contact us in the first instance at secretary@watlingtoncpt.org.uk. You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk or on 0303 123 1113.

Changes to this notice

We will update this privacy notice from time to time. The current version and date are shown at the top of this page. Where changes are significant, we will notify subscribers by email.
